# Plan — Loom (new game, PLANT run, v0.26.0)

_2026-07-24. A genuinely new verb: **weave / interlace**. The collection's 17th game and
its first **weaving** mechanic — you're not steering, timing, aiming, metering, swinging,
remembering, guarding, herding or sorting, you're **choosing over/under at each thread and
alternating to interlace**._

## Why it's distinct

Sluice already owns sort/route (send a falling spark to its matching channel). Polarity owns
flip-match (match your own state to a gate). Loom is neither: the pegs have no "right side"
of their own — the *weave* is defined **relative to your own previous pass**. Interlacing
(alternating over↔under) is the verb; the reads (beads to grab, barbs to dodge) are layered
on an absolute top/bottom field. No other game is about building an interlaced pattern.

## The mechanic (≈3s to grasp)

A shuttle lays the **weft** rightward toward a fixed loom line; **warp pegs** stream in. One
input toggles the weft **over** (top) / **under** (bottom). When a peg reaches the line, the
weft's current side locks for that peg.

- **Interlace** = this side ≠ last side (you alternated) → proper weave.
- **Float** = same side as last → a flaw; resets the multiplier to ×1.
- **Bead** on your side → +bonus points (a collectible; misses cost nothing).
- **Barb** on your side → a **snag**: a life lost (3 snags end the run), combo reset.

## Depth inside the one verb (ships from birth)

1. **No plateau** — `speedOf` is a smooth asymptote on pegs woven (never goes dead-flat).
2. **Hidden tech — Cinch:** toggling to the interlacing side at the *last instant*
   (`CINCH_TICKS` window) grows the multiplier (×2…×9) + a flat bonus; alternating *early*
   is safe but neutral (no growth). Taught nowhere — the deep line is to commit each toggle
   late, which is scary because a mis-time floats or snags. (Polarity's precise/safe tension,
   re-expressed as over/under.)
3. **Reversal — Sheen:** 3 cinches in a row → ~5s where every point doubles (the precise
   hand becomes the greedy one).
4. **Secret stage — Gossamer** past Brocade (220 woven), on no start screen.

## Varied structure + progression

A run is a seeded sequence of named **drafts** (`FORMATIONS`, `minStage`-gated so climbing
stages opens the pool; `weight(stage)` biases late runs toward the hard ones):

- **Plain** (0) — alternating beads, no barbs: the clean on-ramp.
- **Basket** (0) — beads clustered on one side: the **greed window** (float to grab a run).
- **Chevron** (1) — diagonal beads, barb on the empty side (clean interlacing stays safe).
- **Herringbone** (1) — barbs threaten the interlacing side, forcing hard float/risk reads.
- **Sateen** (2) — sparse, long gaps, long float temptations + a barb sting.
- **Snarl** (3) — the dense crescendo.

Stages (keyed on woven): **Warp → Weft → Twill → Damask → Brocade → (secret) Gossamer**.

green tests before ship; previewed before release; simple-but-deep — cut anything that
