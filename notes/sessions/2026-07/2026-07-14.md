### The design problem Poise posed
Poise couldn't take the usual treatment. Every other game varies a **spawn wave** (a queue of gates,
slabs, motes, anchors…), but in Poise **only one target is ever alive**. There is no wave. The flat
generator was a single uniform random point in ±`SPAWN_RANGE`, re-rolled by a `TARGET_TRIES`
rejection loop if it landed on the ball — textureless, so a run was a shapeless hunt with no build.

The varied unit had to be the thing that actually gives a Poise run its shape: **the path the
targets walk you along the beam**. Hence **routes**:

| route | minStage | notable | what it asks of you |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scatter | 0 | — | the loose calm on-ramp (the old generator, tamed) |
| Pendulum | 0 | — | long even sweeps across the fulcrum; brake at each end |
| **Cradle** | 1 | ✦ | **the greed window** — shortest legal hop, always *inward*. The easiest, safest points in the game, on purpose |
| Feint | 1 | ✦ | tight reversals near the middle — short distances, brutal braking, because momentum carries *through* the catch |
| Creep | 2 | ✦ | the walk out: targets step outward one at a time, safe middle → lip |
| The Brink | 3 | ✦ | a run of targets against **one** lip — live out there and hold it. A hover, not a traverse |
| The Reel | 4 | ✦ | the Tempest crescendo: lip-to-lip swings on the heaviest beam you've earned |

`minStage` gates each → climbing the stages **opens the pool** (calm share >75% at Steady → <40% at
Tempest, pinned by a test). Notable routes flash a quiet `#formCue`; the calm ones are silent, so a
first-timer never meets a name cue.

### Two things worth remembering

**1. A new spec vocabulary — because a Poise target is placed *relative to a live ball*.**
Specs come in two forms, resolved by a new pure `placeSpec(cfg, spec, ballPos, rng)`:
- `{f}` — **absolute**: a signed fraction of `SPAWN_RANGE`.
- `{mode:'near', f}` — **relative**: the shortest legal hop, always **inward** (toward the fulcrum).

The `near` mode is what makes **Cradle** a genuine *gift* rather than "slightly closer randomness" —
it is structurally the easiest target the game can produce, and it never points you at a lip. Worth
copying the idea: when a game's varied unit is placed against live state, give the spec vocabulary a
**relative mode**; it buys you a real greed window for free.

`placeSpec` also **guarantees by construction** (for any `ballPos` in [-1, 1]) that a target is
inside ±`SPAWN_RANGE` **and** ≥ `MIN_TARGET_DIST` from the ball. That is a strict *strengthening* of
the old `TARGET_TRIES` rejection loop, which could exhaust its 24 attempts and drop a target on top
of the ball — a free catch, and a latent frame-one bug. `TARGET_TRIES` is retired.

**2. The plateau fix (the real depth win).** Poise's difficulty came *only* from `GRAV_STEP`, keyed
on the **stage index** — and the stages stop at Tempest (score 50). Past 50 the beam never got
heavier again: the whole ceiling was visible in ~2 minutes. That is exactly the stagnation the owner
flagged. Gravity now *also* rides a smooth **asymptote** on the raw score (`gravScale`: ×1 →
`GRAV_SCALE_MAX` 1.22, half-travelled at `GRAV_SCALE_K` = 70 — always creeping, never arriving) on
top of the stage steps, and the product is **hard-capped** at `GRAV_HARD_MAX` (0.0040) so difficulty
stays honest and bounded. Two regressions pin it: it keeps climbing past the last stage, and it
never breaks the cap.

Same shape as Loft's fix (v0.22.1) — and it's now clear this is a **general pattern**: any game
whose difficulty is keyed on a *stage index* plateaus the moment the last stage is entered. Worth
checking the rest of the collection for it.
