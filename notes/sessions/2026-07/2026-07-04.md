- **Shipped a new, mechanically-distinct game: Symmetry** (`games/symmetry/`). Verb =
  **mirror coordination** — a genuinely fresh verb (all prior verbs: steer, time-a-catch,
  thrust, flip-match, aim-and-bounce, precision-stack, keep-aloft, balance). One control,
  the *spread*, drives two catchers that are locked in a mirror about a centre line
  (spread 0 = both at centre, 1 = both at the edges). Orbs fall on both sides at various
  lanes; a catcher only grabs an orb whose lane matches the spread. Because the pair
  mirrors, you frequently **cannot save both sides at once** — a left orb near centre and
  a right orb near the edge demand two different spreads, so you must choose. That forced
  tradeoff is the core fun. The relief valve is the **twin**: a gold-ringed mirrored pair
  at one lane on both sides — line up one spread and you catch both for a bonus point, so
  the read is "hold for the twin or chase this single and sacrifice the far side?". Miss
  three orbs and the run ends; a **combo** builds while you keep catching. Escalation =
  orbs fall faster (`fallSpeedOf`) and spawn thicker (`spawnInterval`) by stage. Full
  Growth stack: stages (Mirror → Reflection → Twin → Kaleidoscope → Singularity) + HUD
  chip + field tint + stage beat; meta (`symmetry.meta`, 9 skill-safe badges, run-report
  + near-miss line, legacy `symmetry.best` preserved). Pure core (normalised lanes/spread,
  seedable RNG), **23 tests** — incl. the frame-one guard (empty field, first orb
  scheduled out), the twin all-or-nothing bonus, catch/miss + combo reset, and a
  determinism check. Bespoke `icon.png` generated (two mirrored orbs + centre axis +
  catcher pills). Previewed in Chrome: start screen + gameplay render clean, no console
  errors, no dead screen.
