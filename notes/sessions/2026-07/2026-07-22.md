## v0.25.2 — Poise: the STILL, EQUILIBRIUM, and the secret stage

**Why Poise.** With the varied-structure rollout complete, the lead GROW lever is
`reference/depth-inside-the-mechanic.md`. Four games still lacked the layer — Poise
(born 2026-07-03), Symmetry, Arc and Sluice. Poise is the **oldest** of them *and* was
already 1 of 4 items in (the no-plateau gravity asymptote landed with its varied-structure
run, v0.22.2), so finishing it was the highest-value, lowest-risk advance — exactly the
Loft precedent from v0.24.14.

**The design question: what advanced technique already exists inside "tilt"?**
Poise's core rule is that *the ball keeps its momentum through a catch*. That makes the
instinctive play obvious — fling the ball at the target, let inertia carry it, chase the
next one. The opposite is a genuine, unrewarded skill: **carry real speed across the beam,
brake hard, and arrive on the target with the ball dead still.** It is emergent from the
existing physics, needs no new control, is invisible unless you look for it, and is a true
second-order reversal (the *calm* line becomes the *greedy* one — the Polarity shape, in
Poise's own vocabulary, and the thing the game is literally named after).

**The anti-farm clause is the design.** "Arrive slow" alone is farmable: creep the whole
beam and every catch is a still. So a still also requires the approach to have **peaked**
at `STILL_PEAK` — proof you travelled and braked, not that you dawdled. `stepBall` keeps
the watermark; a catch resets it. This is what makes it a technique rather than patience,
and it's the clause the test suite leans on hardest.

**Tuning was done against a bot, not by feel.** Two throwaway controllers over 15 seeded
runs each:

| controller | catches | stills | rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| naive chaser (drive at the target) | 361 | **0** | 0% |
| deliberate braker (stop short, creep in) | 651 | 83 | ~13% |

Zero-by-accident is the important number: a first-timer's game is completely untouched
(*safe to not know*), and the bonus can never be stumbled into. ~13% for a crude bang-bang
braker means a human playing *for* it will chain three often enough that Equilibrium is a
real, recurring prize rather than a myth. `STILL_VEL` went 0.004 → 0.006 on the back of
this (0.004 made a 3-chain essentially unreachable for the bot).

**Shipped:**

- `STILL_VEL` / `STILL_PEAK` / `STILL_BONUS` — the tech. Gold bloom + `Still ×N` cue.
- `EQ_TRIGGER` 3 / `EQ_TICKS` 300 / `EQ_MULT` 2 — **Equilibrium**: ~5s where every point
  doubles, beam + target burning gold (colour only — geometry, physics and the catch
  window are untouched, so records stay honest). The triggering catch is never doubled.
- **The Eye** — a sixth stage at score 120, `secret: true`. The start tip's stage ladder
  was deleted so nothing spoils it; reaching it *is* the reveal (gold toast + a kick).
- 3 badges (9 → 12): `still`, `equilibrium`, `the-eye`. `totals.stills` added to the meta,
  upgrading losslessly from legacy saves.

**The one structural change worth flagging:** `score` now means *points* and a new
`catches` counts targets. Bonuses would otherwise have quietly inflated the three
"catch N in a run" badges, so those now test `catches` — there's a test that pins exactly
that (30 points off 12 catches must not claim "catch 25"). `nearMissLine` speaks in points
to match.

**Tests:** +14 (42 → **56**); collection **718/718** green. The new section pins the
constants, both catch kinds, the anti-farm case, the watermark's rise-and-reset, the
streak break, the un-doubled trigger, the window ageing out, the frame-one guard, the
secret stage's uniqueness and position, the catches-not-points badge rule, and a full
seeded run's counter consistency.

## Two bugs the preview caught (both fixed in Poise)

Previewing from the **built `_site/`** (per the v0.25.1 lesson) with the run driven from the
page, three depth moments were checked live: a Still (gold bloom + cue), Equilibrium (beam and
target burning gold), and the secret **The Eye** stage chip + tint. Both problems below were
invisible until the game-over card was actually on screen:

1. **The game-over headline was an invisible bar.** `.title` sets `background-clip:text`, but
   `#gameover .title` / `.title.record` then use the **`background` shorthand**, which resets
   `background-clip` to `border-box` — so "New best balance" rendered as a solid gradient
   rectangle with transparent text. **Every one of the 16 games has this**, so it's a
   collection-wide bug; fixed in Poise, logged in `plans/next-steps.md` for a housekeeping run
   (the fix is two lines per game, but the standing rule means previewing 15 more game-over
   screens, which is a run of its own).
2. **The badge column overflowed short viewports.** Poise now has 12 badges, and a big run can
   earn many at once — the uncapped column pushed the score and title off the top of the panel.
   Capped at `34vh` with a thin mint scrollbar; the whole card fits again.
